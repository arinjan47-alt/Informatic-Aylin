Somtam Thai

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded green papaya
  • 1 small tomato, cut into wedges
  • 10–12 green beans, cut into 2–3 cm pieces
  • 1–2 cloves garlic
  • 2–3 bird’s eye chilies
  • 1–2 tbsp dried shrimp
  • 1–2 tbsp roasted peanuts
  • 1 ½ tbsp palm sugar
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 ½ tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp tamarind juice

Directions:

  • 1. Pound the aromatics: Add garlic and chilies to the mortar. Lightly pound until crushed (don’t overdo it).
  • 2. Add dried shrimp & long beans: Add dried shrimp and long beans. Pound gently to bruise them so flavors release.
  • 3. Add tomatoes: Add tomato wedges and pound lightly until juices come out.
  • 4. Season the salad: Add the seasoning ingredients: Palm sugar (dissolve by gently pounding), Fish sauce, Lime juice, Tamarind juice (if using), Mix well with the pestle and spoon.
  • 5. Add papaya & peanuts: Add the shredded papaya. Toss and pound very lightly — just enough to help the papaya absorb the dressing. Add roasted peanuts and mix.
  • 6. Taste & adjust: A balanced Somtam Thai should be: spicy + sour + salty + slightly sweet. Adjust with more lime, fish sauce, or palm sugar as needed.
